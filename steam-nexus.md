## Concept

STEAM Nexus explores spatial organization as a **rhizome** rather than a hierarchy: a design where science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics activities are woven into a single connective structure with no single "main" entrance, corridor, or axis of authority. Instead of a conventional plan organized around a hierarchy of primary and secondary spaces, the building is conceived as a network of interconnected nodes — echoing how interdisciplinary learning itself moves laterally between subjects rather than along a fixed sequence.

This is part of a broader interest in what I think of as **quantum spatiality**: treating architectural space less like a fixed container and more like a field of possible configurations that respond to how it's used.
