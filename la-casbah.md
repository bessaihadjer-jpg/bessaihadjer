## Context

The Casbah of Algiers is a UNESCO World Heritage Site: a dense, historic urban fabric of Ottoman-era housing, narrow stepped streets, and courtyard typologies overlooking the Bay of Algiers. Its irregular, organically grown morphology makes it a rich case study for urban analysis methods that go beyond conventional zoning-based reading of cities.
